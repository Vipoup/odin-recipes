Whooper (no cheese)

Description

When you drive through Burger King, you want a Whopper Junior with onion rings. Perhaps a double whopper with no cheese because you’re lactose intolerant. However mishaps happen, and you get a whopper jr with hella cheese. Not only is your asshole ruined, but so is their toilet. To avoid that, this is the perfect solution, just don’t order from Burger King. In this recipe, I’ll show you how to recreate their whooper without the cheese.

“I’m in the drive-through of Burger King. Man, they just gave me a Whopper jr with hella cheese… But why they gone put cheese on everything.” -SaintBeDa 2020

Ingredients

  • 1 sesame-seed hamburger bun
  • 1/4 lb. ground beef
  • 1/2teaspoon salt and black pepper
  • 3 dill pickle slices
  • 1 teaspoon ketchup
  • 3-4 sliced onion rings
  • 2 tomato slices
  • 1/4 cup chopped iceberg foot lettuce (see Burger King foot lettuce recipe here)
  • 2 teaspoons mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon ketchup

Steps

  1. Prepare your Burger King foot lettuce
  2. Season the Ground Beef: Start by seasoning your ground beef. Use 1/2 teaspoon of salt and some black pepper to enhance the flavor of the meat. The salt helps to make it taste barely unseasoned. 😫 👌 😋
  3. orm Patties: After seasoning, form the beef into thin flat patties. You don’t want them too THICC ™ because we know you can’t handle that.
  4. Butter and Toast Buns: Next, prepare your hamburger buns. Butter the inside of those buns gently. Toast both halves of the bun in a skillet over medium heat until they are lightly browned. 🥵 The toasting adds CRONCH and prevents the buns from becoming soggy once the burger is assembled. 🤤 Set those beautiful buns aside when finished.
  5. reheat Grill: Heat your grill to medium-high. This temperature is optimal for cooking burgers as it sears the outside quickly, locking in juices while ensuring the inside is cooked to a safe temperature.
  6. Cook the Patties: Place the burger patties on the grill and cook them for 2 to 3 minutes per side. The exact time may vary depending on how THICC ™ you made your patties and the desired level of doneness. While cooking, sprinkle a dash of salt on the burgers. This additional salt during cooking helps make it taste not plain.
  7. Assemble the burgers: Build your burger by placing the cooked meat on the bottom bun. Layer 3 to 4 dill pickle slices, 3 to 4 onion slices, and 2 to 3 tomato slices on the meat. These ingredients can be changed to have it your way. Squirt a small amount of ketchup over these, adding a classic, sweet, and tangy burger taste. Add some lettuce for a crisp, fresh element of foot fungus umami. Finally, spread mayonnaise on the inside of the top bun; mayonnaise adds a creamy texture 💦 and balances the other flavors. Place the top bun on your burger.
